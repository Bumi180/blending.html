My Three-Layer Blending Philosophy

When I first started creating my own tea blends, I found myself overwhelmed by endless possibilities. The breakthrough came when I was looking for perfume. Catching a whiff of a complex, layered fragrance, it suddenly clicked. Tea blending follows the same principles as perfume making.

Base Notes: The Foundation

Just like base notes are the foundation of fragrance, the health benefits form the foundation of my tea blends. These are the deeply therapeutic herbs that do the real work. The chamomile for sleep, the ginger for digestion, the tulsi for stress relief.

  • The largest portion of the blend (40-60%)
  • The reason you're drinking the tea
  • The herbs with the most research behind them
  • Often milder in taste but powerful in effect

Middle Notes: The Character

In tea, these are the flavors that make you want to keep sipping—the warming spices, the floral hints, the earthy undertones that give personality to your blend.

  • The character of your tea (20-40%)
  • What makes each blend unique and memorable
  • The bridge between therapeutic and delicious
  • The flavors that linger pleasantly on your palate

Top Notes: The Harmony

These are the accent herbs that tie everything together. The pop of citrus peel, the warming touch of cardamom, the subtle complexity that makes you pause and think, "What is that lovely note?"

  • The finishing touches (10-20%)
  • Often the most fragrant or intense flavors
  • Used sparingly but with big impact
  • The elements that create harmony between base and middle
